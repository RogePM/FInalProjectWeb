Difficult Trails

Black Mountain Crest Trail

This is one of the toughest hikes in the Southeast, traversing multiple 6,000+ ft. peaks. It’s rugged, steep, and long (12+ miles), but the views from the highest ridgelines east of the Mississippi are unbeatable.

Grandfather Mountain Profile Trail

This trail climbs over 1,800 feet in just a few miles and includes ladders and cables as you near the summit. It’s intense but offers dramatic views and varied terrain from streams to cliffs.

Art Loeb Trail

A multi-day backpacking challenge, the 30-mile Art Loeb Trail is a favorite among experienced hikers. It traverses the Shining Rock Wilderness, with stunning views from peaks like Black Balsam Knob and Tennent Mountain.
